Hiking and Mountaineering

Mountaineering in Tibet is where many expert climbers and mountaineers come to truly test out their skills . For the most part Hiking in Tibet is something that only skilled and experienced climbers should do. The higher altitudes and adverse weather conditions make Tibetan summits one of the hardest places for mountaineering in the world . The harsh climate, complicated terrain, and the effect of the wind can make high-altitude mountaineering a huge risk . The snow covered ground and poor visibility makes it very easy to become disoriented. The weather and conditions can change incredibly quickly, and you must be willing to alter your plans accordingly. Climbers may have to take a different route if a slope is avalanche prone or if a ridge is too windy. One should always review their planned route before setting off whilst in Tibet!

In the spirit of avoiding incidents, one must have the adequate knowledge and skills prior to hiking in Tibet and should always hike with an experienced group of climbers . Highly helpful and experienced mountain tour guides called Sherpas are also a good to consider having on one’s journey.

A good movie to watch prior to your visit to Tibet or just in general which features climbing in Tibet and the Himalayas is ‘Seven Years in Tibet’ which is based off a true story and stars Brad Pitt . Within this movie one can see the extremes of hiking within Tibet and be moved by a touching story.


Top Treks Tibet:

Mount Shishapangma Base Camp Trek:

Mount Shishapangma is one of a mere 14 peaks reaching 8,000 meters in the world. You can make the epic trek up to a 4900-meter elevation to this base camp in as little as a week. If you have a few days free you and the will you can continue to the Advanced Base Camp at 5,400 meters elevation. The hiking here isn’t overly difficult and is mostly on decent pathed trails.

Advanced Base Camp Trek Everest:

This is one of most spectacular treks in Tibet and is also the world’s highest elevation trek reaching 6,340 meters at its highest point. The trek starts at the small monastery of Rongbuk and is only 22km to the Advanced Base camp. During this 22km you are gaining 1,310 meters, which is huge given the already high elevation. This is the same route used by climbers using the North Col to summit Everest.

Saray Kumalo, first black woman from Africa to summit Mt. Everest

East Mount Everest Base Camp - Kangshung Face:

This is an 8-day trek to the seldomly visited Kangshung Face Base Camp which is the eastern base camp for Mount Everest. The trail goes through the Shao La pass (4,915m) and Langma La pass (5,200m) and continues through the beautiful Gama Valley. The trek also winds through an area of rich floral diversity and some of the world’s highest elevation forests before bringing you to the base camp. At the base camp there are great views of Everest, Makalu, and Lhotse . The Kangshung Face is the most dangerous approach to Everest and hence the least used. The magnificent rock wall viewed from the base camp has a vertical face of over 3,350 meters.
