China

Until 1949 , Tibet was an independent peaceful Buddhist nation in the Himalayas which had little contact with the rest of the world. It existed as a rich cultural silo of the Mahāyāna teachings of Buddhism. A strictly non-violent religion was what unified the Tibetans who lived very simply but in harmony for the most part. Despite their precious religion holding them together their distinct language, literature, art, world view and balance with nature kept them unified and moral high. This all changed in 1949 when the contradictory Peaceful Liberation of Tibet took place. The “Peaceful Liberation of Tibet” was the process by which the Peoples Republic of China (PRC) gained control of Tibet and was far from peaceful. A catastrophe occurred. A true crime against humanity .

Since 1949 over a million Tibetans have been killed . Primarily Han Chinese people have resettled in Tibet now making Tibetans become a minority in their own country . Chinese/Mandarin has become the official language. A full-blown cultural genocide has taken place. In 2021 an estimated over 6,000 monasteries and shrines have been destroyed and many more are continually being destroyed. Famines have appeared for the first time in recorded history, natural resources are devastated, and wildlife depleted to extinction. Tibetan culture has almost been fully irradicated within Tibet . The Buddhist religion has been wiped out and bastardized within Tibet by the Chinese.

Compared to pre-1959 levels, only 1/20 monks are still allowed to practice but strictly under the government's watch . The PRC has also replaced Buddhist leaders with members of their liking who should have no say in Tibetan Buddhism which has led to inferior quality of authentic spiritual practice within Tibet.

Exclusively peaceful demonstrations , protests, speeches and writings by nuns, monks, and Tibetan laypeople have resulted in deaths and thousands of arrests . Even just having a photo of The Dalai Lama is considered a detainable offense. These political prisoners are tortured and held in less than adequate conditions, with little to no hope of justice. What is going on in Tibet is much like what China is opposing on the Uyghur Muslims within China as well as the Apartheid system we had in South Africa. What is happening in Tibet is far from Martin Luther King junior’s dream for the world.
